ARE MOST FOREIGN SCHOLARSHIPS AND LINKS LEGAL, IS IT FEASIBLY POSSIBLE FOR THEM TO HELP YOU STUDY ABROAD.

With the issue of feasibility of foreign scholarships, whether they are legit or not OR how true they are, there is a complex way of finding out. With a whole lot of fakes and scams in the system, there is the need for people (especially students) to be very careful when searching or applying for these scholarships. From not having enough knowledge, using malicious sites, through bad agencies to well organized scams to dupe students, there are even more left to discover. Here at Varsity Explorer, we have brought together a narrative to give you insight on what’s really going on and how you can protect yourself by steering clear these dangers and mishaps.

The uprise in students getting duped

There are so many legal and legit scholarships in the system that may be of benefit to many. It is very evident, that these are legit and real. Thousands of students have had a form of support or scholarship to go study abroad. And their testimonies alone are enough to say, there a lot of legit scholarships in the system. And yes, it is very possible to study abroad for any degree at any level. From the undergrad level to post graduate. The educational system abroad (Europe and America) is less stressful and tedious as compared to our educational system back home (Africa), and so, many students resort to travelling abroad to study. This has led to so many fake links and scholarships purposely created to dupe people. Not only 1 but the potential of 2 in every 55 students getting duped is marginally high.

The discovery made: A close discovery made too was, that some of these scholarship schemes are poorly structured leaving you as good as duped, in simple explanation is that “one story is getting the scholarship, the other is travelling abroad”. This scam scheme comes in so many sensitive ways that the applicant cannot see, they are getting duped. Some agencies will help you gain admission or scholarship into prestigious universities abroad, but upon the applicant reaching the final stages, they are charged with exorbitant or extorted a ridiculous amount fee. They let you come in the easy way but let you go the hard way. It’s a case of making the applicant see cana’an without giving them a chance to step there, unless they pay a charged fee. (This compels students to pay so they don’t waste all the efforts they have put in).

Final conclusion

Be keen and alert, but hope on There are ways in which these scams can be easily spotted, and some of these are; scholarships that ask for loan fees before they disburse money to you. Also there is a need to never trust companies that advertise that ‘they guarantee that they will get you a scholarship’. These types of companies will ask for a fee and may even offer a money back guarantee but will never get you a scholarship. Legitimate services will never say they guarantee to get you a scholarship. In some cases, there are fraudsters who cover up their scam by using words like official, government, national or other official sounding names. It is very necessary to search for scholarships yourself, rather than depending on anyone. From writing your d\standardized tests to boarding a plane to leave for your study, there is a need to be well aware of everything yourself. One way to avoid being swindled is to check for scholarships on official government websites or official school websites for information, rather than depending on middle men or miscellaneous sites. The dreams of many, are shattered upon the realization that they have been deceived. Sometimes the carelessness of prospective students of schools abroad, costs them their education there. They end up paying money to people to help them, despite the fact that those demanding the money can be easily identified as scammers or people without anything organized or formal. It is therefore necessary to be very vigilant and to research about everybody or organization of any sort before dealing with them. Better still, one can just do everything in a solo fashion (through important figureheads/right channels in their current universities or from a legal institute).
