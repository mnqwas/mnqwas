THE TRUTH ABOUT STUDYING ABROAD. GOOD OR BAD.

Studying abroad has been the long term dream of a lot of students, especially tertiary students. However, there have been a lot of myths and truths surrounding this. In this write-up, the truth about this, shall be clearly delineated. The whole concept of studying abroad is centered on the fact that schools abroad have better studying and learning conditions than schools in our homeland and also gives more opportunities on the job market. The educational system abroad allows students work and school at the same time, whereas being close to impossible back in our homeland (Africa). Students who manage to do both, end up performing abysmally in one of them, which defeats the purpose. Scholarships and assistantships are some of the major reasons why many students prefer to study abroad. Getting an opportunity to study abroad, either your tuition and accommodation fees being taken care of, would never be rejected by any student in his right mind.

The tedious journey

With scholarships and assistantships being so alluring, they come with a lot obstructions most of the time. For a student from an African country, it is mostly required to take certain standardized tests, to prove youâ€™re worthy of any scholarship that may be awarded to you. And since students from around the world are in a serious competition for limited slots in the universities, and for the fact that each country has a unique educational system, there is the need for a platform that puts all students on the same level to be tested. That is where these international or standardized tests play a major role. These standardized tests are widely claimed to be difficult. They require serious studying and a lot of commitment. The prices of these tests are very high, and so are not attempted by many students. The whole process of studying abroad is a tedious one, but once a person steps foot into their dream school, there is a sense of relief. From acquiring, passports, to visas and plane tickets, there is no cool or funny moment. Failing interviews at embassies has been a major turn-off for many students, leaving them enervated and bleak.

Studying abroad comes with so many benefits, but getting there is usually the biggest hurdle. With this being one side of the whole issue, it is time to explore the other side. People have gained admission into top schools without high GPAs, or even any standard exam like the Graduate Record Exam or TOEFEL OR SAT. Opportunities like these, do come from time to time. These kind of scholarships are usually from Governments of developed countries like the United States, Canada, Russia and etc. Some opportunities do come in the form of exchange programs. Other students just apply and get admission without paying a penny for anything. Their plane tickets are taken care of, accommodation, tuition fees and monthly stipends are all provided without going through any obstructions or hurdles.

The many things forgotten most of the time

Why don't these countries give the scholarship to their own citizens, well there's sleek but sneaky reason why these scholarships are offered: Most of these are in overview of sports and skillset poaching/brain drain from the African continent. A good athlete or sports person stands a better chance as much as a brilliant or needy student in gaining admission or assistantship into or from foreign institute. One thing too is gaining admission there, the other is adapting to stay there. One has to acclimatize to the foreign weather which is generally very cold as opposed to the hot temperate regions in Africa. The food, language, music basically everything it entails in their culture or the foreign lands culture one has to be ready to accept and live with it, not forgetting knowing their laws and also laws governing foreign nationals. Being accepted by people living there too can sometimes be a problem due to social cohesion and common stigma. Most foriegn nationals are easy to work with or bargain salary schemes with even if they are getting underpaid because most need to work to survive or get a job to bring money home. If you are really bent or hoping to study abroad, you should be clear and know for sure that there are thousands plus student dreaming the same like you, so you might want to up your game, eagerness and action in making it a reality. So, the truth about studying abroad has been clearly stated. Even though there are a lot of difficulties, in the process of gaining admission to study abroad and living there, there are easier paths one can take to arrive at the same destination.
